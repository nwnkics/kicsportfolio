Our Project Portfolio

Next-generation wireless networking (NWN) lab at Al-Khawrizmi Institute of Computer Science (KICS) focuses in research and development activities in the areas of wireless sensor networks (WSN), Internet of things (IoT) and software defined networks (SDN). Comprising 1 PHDs and over 7 full-time research associates and undergraduate/postgraduate interns

Acoustic Intelligent Surveillance System

An AI-powered system that uses acoustic sensors and signal processing to detect and analyze sound patterns for real-time security surveillance in urban environments.

2D / 3D Acoustic-Based Target Locating System

Employs acoustic triangulation to locate and map objects in both 2D and 3D spaces, enabling advanced threat detection and precision navigation in complex environments.

Ground Penetrating Radar

Non-invasive subsurface imaging system used for detecting hidden underground structures, utilities, and archaeological artifacts with high accuracy.

Indoor Fire and Smoke Detection System

Utilizes sensors and real-time data analysis to identify early signs of fire and smoke in indoor spaces, enhancing safety in commercial and industrial settings.

Body-Worn Concealed Weapon Detection System

A portable solution integrated with smart sensors to detect hidden metal and non-metal threats on individuals for enhanced security at sensitive locations.

Carbon Sequestration and Plant Growth Monitoring System

Monitors soil carbon levels and vegetation growth using IoT and satellite-linked sensors to support climate change mitigation and sustainable farming.

Digital Rail Drive Assistance

An intelligent assistance system for rail drivers using AI-based hazard detection, track condition monitoring, and optimized driving recommendations.
